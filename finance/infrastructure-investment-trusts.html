Infrastructure Investment Trusts (InvITs)

amith

@amith

Introduction

Roads, highways, electricity lines, mobile towers, and renewable energy plants are very important for a country like India. These things help people travel, businesses grow, and cities develop.

Building such infrastructure costs a lot of money and takes many years. To solve this problem, India introduced a new investment option called Infrastructure Investment Trusts, or InvITs.

What is an Infrastructure Investment Trust?

An InvIT is a way for people to invest money in large infrastructure projects. Instead of buying roads or power lines directly, investors buy small units of an InvIT.

The InvIT owns infrastructure projects that are already working and earning money. This money is then shared with investors regularly.

Simple Example: When you pay toll on a highway, part of that money can go to InvIT investors.

Why India Needs InvITs

  • India needs huge money to build roads, railways, and power systems
  • Banks cannot give loans for very long periods easily
  • Government wants private money to help build infrastructure
  • InvITs attract money from Indian and foreign investors

How an InvIT Works

Sponsor

Company or government body that puts roads or power assets into the InvIT

Trustee

Makes sure investors’ money is safe

Investment Manager

Manages projects and money

Investors

People who buy InvIT units and earn income

How Do Investors Earn Money?

InvITs earn money from tolls, electricity charges, mobile tower rent, and long-term government contracts.

By law, InvITs must give most of this money (at least 90%) to investors. This is usually paid every three or six months.

InvITs in the Indian Stock Market

The following InvITs are listed on Indian stock exchanges like NSE and BSE:

  • IndiGrid Infrastructure Trust – Power transmission lines
  • PowerGrid Infrastructure Investment Trust – Government-backed power assets
  • IRB InvIT Fund – National highways and roads
  • National Highways Infra Trust (NHIT) – Roads owned by NHAI
  • Brookfield India Infrastructure Trust – Telecom towers and digital assets

Benefits of InvITs

  • Regular income like rent
  • Lower risk than starting new projects
  • Helps build the nation
  • Good for long-term investors

Risks to Know

Income can reduce if traffic on roads falls or rules change. Market prices of InvIT units can also go up and down.

Government Rules and Safety

InvITs are regulated by SEBI (India’s market regulator). SEBI makes rules to protect investors and ensure transparency.

Future of InvITs in India

As India builds more highways, renewable energy projects, and digital networks, InvITs are expected to grow and become more popular.

Conclusion

Infrastructure Investment Trusts make it possible for ordinary people to invest in India’s development. They offer steady income and help build the country at the same time.
